Dhyanam

▾
Invocatory Verses — recited before commencing the Stotram
#ShlokaMeaning
VERSE 1 śuklāmbaradharam vishṇum śaśivarṇam chaturbhujam |prasanna-vadanam dhyāyet sarvavighnopa-śāntaye || 1 || We meditate upon Lord Vishnu, who is clad in white, who is of the colour of the moon, who has four arms, and who has a pleasant countenance — for the removal of all obstacles.
VERSE 2 yasyad-virada-vaktrādyāf pārishadyāf paraś śatam |vighnam nighnanti satatam viśvaksenam tamāśraye || 2 || I take refuge in Vishvaksena, whose attendants, headed by the elephant-faced one and numbering more than a hundred, constantly remove all obstacles.
VERSE 3 vyāsam vasishṭha naptāram śaktef pautrama-kalmasham |parāśarātmajam vande śukatātam taponidhim || 3 || I salute Vyasa — grandson of Vasishtha, son of Shakti, son of Parashara, grandfather of Shuka — that sinless treasury of austerity.
VERSE 4 vyāsāya vishṇu rūpāya vyāsarūpāya vishṇave |namo vai bramhanidhaye vāsishṭhāya namo namaha || 4 || Salutations to Vyasa who is in the form of Vishnu, and to Vishnu who is in the form of Vyasa — to that treasure of Brahman, the descendant of Vasishtha, salutations again and again.
VERSE 5 avikārāya śuddhāya nityāya paramātmane |sadaika rūpa rūpāya vishṇave sarvajishṇave || 5 || Salutations to Vishnu — who is unchanging, pure, eternal, the supreme Self, who is always of one form, and who is ever victorious over all.
VERSE 6 yasya smaraṇa-mātreṇa janma-samsāra-bandhanāt |vimuchyate namastasmai vishṇave prabha-vishṇave || 6 || By the mere remembrance of whom one is freed from the bondage of the cycle of births — salutations to that Vishnu, the resplendent Lord.
CHANT om namo vishṇave prabha-vishṇave | Om. Salutations to Vishnu, the resplendent Lord.

Poorvangam

▾
The sacred dialogue between Yudhiṣṭhira and Grandsire Bhīṣma — establishing the glory of Vishnu Sahasranāma (Mahābhārata, Anuśāsana Parva)
#ShlokaMeaning
Śrī Vaiśampāyana Uvācha
VERSE 7 śrutvā dharmān aśesheṇa pāvanāni cha sarvaśaha |yudhishṭhiraś śāntanavam punarevābhya bhāshata || 7 || Sri Vaisampayana said: After hearing in full the Dharmas and all the purifying teachings, Yudhishthira once again addressed Shantanu's son (Bhishma).
Yudhishṭhira Uvācha
VERSE 8 kimekam daivatam loke ? kim vāpyekam parāyaṇam ?stuvantah kam ? kamarchantaf prāpnuyurmānavāś śubham ? || 8 || Yudhishthira asked: Who is the one God in this world? Who is the one supreme refuge? By praising whom, by worshipping whom, do human beings attain what is auspicious?
VERSE 9 ko dharmas sarva-dharmāṇām bhavataf paramo mataha ? |kim japanmuchyate jantur janma samsāra bandhanāt ? || 9 || What, in your opinion, is the greatest Dharma among all Dharmas? By chanting what is a creature freed from the bondage of the cycle of birth and death?
Śrī Bhīshma Uvācha
VERSE 10 jagat-prabhum deva-devam anantam purushottamam |stuvannāma sahasreṇa purushas satatotthitaha || 10 || Bhishma replied: A person who is constantly devoted and who praises the Lord of the Universe, God of Gods, the Infinite, the Supreme Being, with His thousand names —
VERSE 11 tameva chārchayannityam bhaktyā purusha-mavyayam |dhyāyan stuvannama-syamścha yajamānastameva cha || 11 || — who daily worships that imperishable Purusha with devotion, meditating, praising and saluting Him, and making offerings to Him —
VERSE 12 anādi nidhanam vishṇum sarvaloka maheśvaram |lokādhyaksham stuvannityam sarva duhkhātigo bhavet || 12 || — who daily praises Vishnu, who has no beginning or end, who is the great Lord of all worlds and the overseer of the universe — that person is freed from all sorrows.
VERSE 13 bramhaṇyam sarva dharmagñam lokānām kīrti vardhanam |lokanātham mahadbhūtam sarvabhūta bhavodbhavam || 13 || He who is the friend of Brahmanas, who knows all Dharmas, who increases the glory of the worlds, who is the protector of the universe, the great being, and from whom all beings arise —
VERSE 14 esha me sarva dharmāṇām dharmoऽdhika tamomataha |yadbhaktyā puṇḍarīkāksham stavairarchennnaras sadā || 14 || This, in my view, is the greatest of all Dharmas — that a person, with devotion, constantly praises and worships the lotus-eyed Lord with hymns.
VERSE 15 paramam yo mahattejaf paramam yo mahattapaha |paramam yo mahad-bramha paramam yaf parāyaṇam || 15 || He who is of supreme great splendour, who is the supreme great austerity, who is the supreme great Brahman, and who is the supreme refuge —
VERSE 16 pavitrāṇām pavitram yo mangaḷānām cha mangaḷam |daivatam devatānām cha bhūtānām yoऽvyayaf pitā || 16 || — He who is the purest of the pure, the most auspicious of all that is auspicious, the God of all gods, and the imperishable Father of all beings —
VERSE 17 yatas sarvāṇi bhūtāni bhavantyādi yugāgame |yasmimścha pralayam yānti punareva yugakshaye || 17 || — from whom all beings arise at the dawn of creation, and into whom they dissolve again at the end of each age —
VERSE 18 tasya loka pradhānasya jagannāthasya bhūpate |vishṇornāma sahasram me śṛṇu pāpa bhayāpaham || 18 || O King, listen from me to the thousand names of that Lord of the Universe, foremost among all, the Lord Vishnu — which remove fear and sin.
VERSE 19 yāni nāmāni gauṇāni vikhyātāni mahātmanaha |ṛshibhif parigītāni tāni vakshyāmi bhūtaye || 19 || The names of the great Lord that are well-known by their qualities, and which have been sung by the sages — those names I shall now recite for the welfare of all.
VERSE 20 ṛshirnāmnām sahasrasya vedavyāso mahāmunihi ||chhandoऽnushṭup tathā devo bhagavān devakīsutaha || 20 || The seer (Rishi) of the thousand names is the great sage Veda Vyasa; the metre is Anushtup; and the deity is Bhagavan, the son of Devaki.
VERSE 21 amṛtāmśūdbhavo bījam śaktirdevakinandanaha |trisāmā hṛdayam tasya śāntyarthe viniyujyate || 21 || Amritamshoodbhava is the seed (bija); Devakinandana is the power (shakti); Trisama is its heart — and all this is employed for the sake of peace.
VERSE 22 vishṇum jishṇum mahāvishṇum prabhavishṇum maheśvaram ||anekarūpa daityāntam namāmi purushottamam || 22 || I bow to Vishnu, the Victor, the Great Vishnu, the Resplendent Vishnu, the Great Lord — who has many forms, who is the destroyer of the Asuras, and who is the Supreme Being.

This recitation is for the pleasure of Sri MahaVishnu.

Uttara Bhāgam — Phala Śrutihi (Benefits of Recitation)
VERSE 1 itīdam kīrtanīyasya keśavasya mahātmanaha |nāmnām sahasram divyānā maśesheṇa prakīrtitam || 1 || Thus have been fully recited the thousand divine names of the great-souled Kesava, worthy of praise.
VERSE 2 ya idam śṛṇuyānnityam yaśchāpi parikīrtayet ||nāśubham prāpnuyāt kiñchitsoऽmutreha cha mānavaha || 2 || The person who hears this daily or who recites it — that human being will not attain any inauspicious thing here or in the hereafter.
VERSE 3 vedāntago brāmhaṇas syāt kshatriyo vijayī bhavet |vaiśyo dhanasamṛddhas syāch chhūdras sukhamavāpnuyāt || 3 || A Brahmin will attain mastery of the Vedas; a Kshatriya will be victorious; a Vaishya will be richly prosperous; and a Shudra will attain happiness.
VERSE 4 dharmārthī prāpnuyād dharma marthārthī chārthamāpnuyāt |kāmāna vāpnuyāt kāmī prajārthī chāpnuyāt prajām || 4 || One who seeks Dharma will attain Dharma; one who seeks wealth will attain wealth; one who seeks pleasures will attain them; and one who seeks progeny will be blessed with children.
VERSE 5 bhaktimān yas sadotthāya śuchis tadgatamānasaha |sahasram vāsudevasya nāmnāmetat prakīrtayet || 5 || The devotee who rises daily with a pure heart and mind focused on Vasudeva, and recites these thousand names —
VERSE 6 yaśaf prāpnoti vipulam ñātiprādhānyameva cha |achalām śriyamāpnoti śreyaf prāpnotyanuttamam || 6 || — will attain abundant fame, prominence among his kinsmen, lasting prosperity, and the supreme good.
VERSE 7 na bhayam kvachidāpnoti vīryam tejaścha vindati |bhavatyarogo dyutimān balarūpa guṇānvitaha || 7 || Such a person is never overcome by fear, attains valour and brilliance, becomes free from disease, radiant, endowed with strength, beauty and all virtues.
VERSE 8 rogārto muchyate rogād- baddho muchyeta bandhanāt |bhayān-muchyeta bhītastu muchyetāpanna āpadaha || 8 || One afflicted with illness is freed from illness; one who is bound is freed from bondage; one who is afraid is freed from fear; and one caught in calamity is freed from peril.
VERSE 9 durgāṇyatitaratyāśu purushaf purushottamam |stuvannāma sahasreṇa nityam bhakti samanvitaha || 9 || One who praises the Supreme Purusha with these thousand names daily and with devotion swiftly crosses all difficulties.
VERSE 10 vāsudevāśrayo martyo vāsudeva parāyaṇaha |sarvapāpa viśuddhātmā yāti bramha sanātanam || 10 || The mortal who takes refuge in Vasudeva and is devoted to Vasudeva, with a soul purified of all sins, attains the eternal Brahman.
VERSE 11 na vāsudeva bhaktānā maśubham vidyate kvachit |janma mṛtyu jarā vyādhi bhayam naivopajāyate || 11 || For devotees of Vasudeva, no inauspiciousness exists anywhere. Fear of birth, death, old age and disease does not arise for them.
VERSE 12 imam stavamadhīyānaś śraddhā bhakti samanvitaha |yujyetātma sukhakshānti śrīdhṛti smṛti kīrtibhihi || 12 || One who studies this hymn with faith and devotion is united with inner joy, patience, prosperity, fortitude, memory and glory.
VERSE 13 na krodho na cha mātsaryam na lobho nāśubhāmatihi |bhavanti kṛta puṇyānām bhaktānām purushottame || 13 || Anger, jealousy, greed and evil thoughts do not arise in those blessed devotees of the Supreme Being who have accumulated merit.
VERSE 14 dyaus sa chandrārka nakshatrā kham diśo bhūrmahodadhihi |vāsudevasya vīryeṇa vidhṛtāni mahātmanaha || 14 || The sky with moon, sun and stars; the space, the directions, the earth and the great oceans — all are upheld by the power of the great-souled Vasudeva.
VERSE 15 sasurāsura gandharvam sayakshoraga rākshasam |jagadvaśe vartatedam kṛshṇasya sa charācharam || 15 || This entire world — with gods, asuras, gandharvas, yakshas, nagas and rakshasas, and all that is moving and unmoving — is under the sway of Krishna.
VERSE 16 indriyāṇi manobuddhis sattvam tejo balam dhṛtihi |vāsudevātma kānyāhuhu kshetram kshetragña eva cha || 16 || The wise declare that the senses, mind, intellect, being, splendour, strength, fortitude, the body (kshetra) and the soul of the body (kshetrajna) — all are forms of Vasudeva.
VERSE 17 sarvāgamānā māchāraf prathamam parikalpate |āchāra prabhavo dharmo dharmasya prabhurachyutaha || 17 || Right conduct is held first among all the scriptures. Dharma is born of right conduct; and the Lord of Dharma is Achyuta.
VERSE 18 ṛshayaf pitaro devā mahābhūtāni dhātavaha |jangamā jangamam chedam jagannārāyaṇodbhavam || 18 || The sages, the ancestors, the gods, the great elements, the dhatus, and all that is moving and unmoving in this universe — all arise from Narayana, the Lord of the world.
VERSE 19 yogogñānam tathā sānkhyam vidyāś śilpādikarma cha |vedāś śāstrāṇi vigñānametat sarvam janārdanāt || 19 || Yoga, knowledge, Sankhya philosophy, the arts, the sciences with their practical applications, the Vedas, the scriptures and all learning — all this comes from Janardhana.
VERSE 20 eko vishṇur-mahad-bhūtam pṛthagbhūtā nyanekaśaha |trīllokān vyāpya bhūtātmā bhunkte viśvabhugavyayaha || 20 || The one Vishnu is the great being; the diverse beings are He manifested as many. Pervading the three worlds as the soul of all beings, the imperishable Lord of all enjoys the universe.
VERSE 21 imam stavam bhagavato vishṇor-vyāsena kīrtitam |paṭhedya ichchhet-purushaś śreyaf prāptum sukhāni cha || 21 || He who desires to attain the supreme good and happiness should recite this hymn of Lord Vishnu, as sung by Vyasa.
VERSE 22 viśveśvara majam devam jagataf prabhumavyayam |bhajanti ye pushkarāksham na te yānti parābhavam || 22 ||na te yānti parābhavam om nama iti | Those who worship the lotus-eyed Lord — the Lord of the Universe, the unborn, the imperishable Master of the world — will never come to ruin. Om Nama.
Arjuna Uvācha
VERSE 23 padmapatra viśālāksha padmanābha surottama |bhaktānā manuraktānām trātā bhava janārdana || 23 || Arjuna said: O Janardhana, with eyes wide as lotus petals, with a lotus on your navel, O best of gods — be the protector of your devoted and loving devotees.
Śrī Bhagavān Uvācha
VERSE 24 yo mām nāma sahasreṇa stotumichchati pāṇḍava |soऽhamekena ślokena stuta eva na samśayaha || 24 ||stuta eva na samśaya om nama iti | The Lord said: O Pandava, whoever wishes to praise me with the thousand names — I am just as pleased by one verse. There is no doubt. Om Nama.
Vyāsa Uvācha
VERSE 25 vāsanād vāsudevasya vāsitam bhuvanatrayam |sarvabhūta nivāsoऽsi vāsudeva namoऽstu te || 25 ||śrīvāsudeva namoऽstuta om nama iti | Vyasa said: The three worlds are pervaded by the fragrance of Vasudeva. O Vasudeva, you are the dwelling of all beings — salutations to you. Om Nama.
Pārvatyuvācha
VERSE 26 kenopāyena laghunā vishṇor-nāma sahasrakam |paṭhyate paṇḍitair-nityam śrotumichchhāmyaham prabho || 26 || Parvati said: O Lord, I wish to know — by what easy means do the wise daily recite the thousand names of Vishnu? Please tell me.
Īśvara Uvācha
VERSE 27 🔁 Chant 3×śrīrāma rāma rāmeti rame rāme manorame |sahasranāma tattulyam rāmanāma varānane || 27 ||śrīrāma nāma varānana om nama iti | Lord Shiva said: O beautiful-faced one, I play in the name Rama, Rama, Rama — ever delighting in it. The name Rama equals the thousand names. Om Nama.
Brahmā Uvācha
VERSE 28 namoऽstvanantāya sahasramūrtaye sahasrapādākshi śirorubāhave |sahasranāmne purushāya śāśvate sahasrakoṭi yugadhāriṇe namaha || 28 ||śrī sahasrakoṭi yugadhāriṇe om nama iti | Brahma said: Salutations to the Infinite — who has a thousand forms, a thousand feet, eyes, heads and arms; who has a thousand names; who is the eternal Purusha; who bears a thousand crore of ages. Salutations. Om Nama.
Sañjaya Uvācha
VERSE 29 yatra yogeśvarah kṛshṇo yatra pārtho dhanurdharaha |tatra śrīr-vijayo bhūtir- dhruvā nītir-matir-mama || 29 || Sanjaya said: Where there is Krishna, the Lord of Yoga, and where there is Arjuna the wielder of the bow — there, in my firm opinion, will be prosperity, victory, glory and righteousness.
Śrī Bhagavān Uvācha
VERSE 30 ananyāś chintayanto mām ye janāf paryupāsate |teshām nityābhiyuktānām yogakshemam vahāmyaham || 30 || The Lord said: For those who worship me with single-minded devotion, thinking of none other, I personally carry what they lack and preserve what they have.
VERSE 31 paritrāṇāya sādhūnām vināśāya cha dushkṛtām ||dharma samsthāpanārthāya sambhavāmi yuge yuge || 31 || For the protection of the good, for the destruction of the wicked, and for the firm establishment of Dharma — I am born in every age.
VERSE 32 🔁 Chant 2×ārtā vishaṇṇāś śithilāścha bhītāh ghoreshu cha vyādhishu vartamānāha |sankīrtya nārāyaṇa śabdamātram vimukta duhkhās sukhino bhavantu || 32 || May those who are suffering, despairing, weakened or afraid, and those afflicted with terrible diseases — be freed from sorrow and attain happiness, by simply chanting the name of Narayana.
VERSE 33 kāyena vāchā manasendri yairvā buddhyātmanā vā prakṛtes svabhāvāt |karomi yadyat-sakalam parasmai nārāyaṇāyeti samarpayāmi || 33 || Whatever I do with my body, speech, mind, senses, intellect, soul or by the natural tendencies of my nature — I offer all of that completely to the Supreme Lord Narayana.
Kshama Prārthanā — Prayer for Forgiveness
