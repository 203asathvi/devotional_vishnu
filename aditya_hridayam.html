Āditya Hṛdayam

आदित्यहृदयम्
The Heart of Āditya — the Sun God
Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa · Yuddha Kāṇḍa · Sarga 107 · Revealed by Agastya Maharṣi to Śrī Rāma
✦

Context — The Battle Scene

▾
From the Yuddha Kāṇḍa of the Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa — On the battlefield of Lanka, Śrī Rāma stood exhausted and absorbed in deep thought, facing the mighty Rāvaṇa. Seeing this, the divine sage Agastya descended from the heavens in the company of gods and taught Rāma this eternal secret hymn — the Āditya Hṛdayam — to bestow victory.

Introduction — Verses 1–5

▾
Agastya Maharṣi speaks to Śrī Rāma
VERSE 1–2
ततो युद्धपरिश्रान्तं समरे चिन्तया स्थितम् ।
रावणं चाग्रतो दृष्ट्वा युद्धाय समुपस्थितम् ॥ १ ॥

दैवतैश्च समागम्य द्रष्टुमभ्यागतो रणम् ।
उपागम्याब्रवीद्राम-मगस्त्यो भगवान् ऋषिः ॥ २ ॥
tato yuddhapariśrāntaṃ samare cintayā sthitam |
rāvaṇaṃ cāgrato dṛṣṭvā yuddhāya samupasthitam || 1 ||

daivataiśca samāgamya draṣṭumabhyāgato raṇam |
upāgamyābravīdrāma-magastyo bhagavān ṛṣiḥ || 2 ||
Beholding Śrī Rāma standing absorbed in deep thought on the battlefield, exhausted by the fight and facing Rāvaṇa who was duly prepared for war, the glorious sage Agastya — who had come with the gods to witness the battle — now approached and spoke to Rāma as follows.
VERSE 3
राम राम महाबाहो श्रृणु गुह्यं सनातनम् ।
येन सर्वानरीन् वत्स समरे विजयिष्यसि ॥ ३ ॥
rāma rāma mahābāho śṛṇu guhyaṃ sanātanam |
yena sarvānarīn vatsa samare vijayiṣyasi || 3 ||
O Rāma! O mighty-armed Rāma! Listen carefully to this eternal secret, by which, O my child, you shall conquer all your enemies on the battlefield.
VERSE 4
आदित्यहृदयं पुण्यं सर्वशत्रु-विनाशनम् ।
जयावहं जपेन्नित्य-मक्षय्यं परमं शिवम् ॥ ४ ॥
ādityahṛdayaṃ puṇyaṃ sarvaśatru-vināśanam |
jayāvahaṃ japennitya-makṣayyaṃ paramaṃ śivam || 4 ||
By chanting the Āditya Hṛdayam — this meditation of the Sun in the heart — which is most auspicious and highly beneficial, you shall be victorious in battle. This holy hymn dedicated to the Sun God destroys all enemies, brings victory, and bestows permanent happiness and auspiciousness.
VERSE 5
सर्वमङ्गल-माङ्गल्यं सर्वपापप्रणाशनम् ।
चिन्ताशोक-प्रशमन-मायुर्वर्धन-मुत्तमम् ॥ ५ ॥
sarvamaṅgala-māṅgalyaṃ sarvapāpapraṇāśanam |
cintāśoka-praśamana-māyurvardhana-muttamam || 5 ||
This supreme prayer is the best amongst all auspicious verses. It destroys all sins, dispels all doubts, alleviates worry, sorrow, anxiety and anguish, and increases the longevity of life. It is a guarantee of complete prosperity.

The Hymn — Verses 6–21

▾
The glories and names of Sūrya Bhagavān — the Sun God
VERSE 6
रश्मिमन्तं समुद्यन्तं देवासुर-नमस्कृतम् ।
पूजयस्व विवस्वन्तं भास्करं भुवनेश्वरम् ॥ ६ ॥
raśmimantaṃ samudyantaṃ devāsura-namaskṛtam |
pūjayasva vivasvantaṃ bhāskaraṃ bhuvaneśvaram || 6 ||
Worship the Sun God — the ruler of worlds and lord of the universe — who is crowned with effulgent rays, who rises at the horizon bringing light, and who is revered by both the gods (devas) and demons (asuras) alike.
VERSE 7
सर्वदेवात्मको ह्येष तेजस्वी रश्मि-भावनः ।
एष देवासुरगणान् लोकान् पाति गभस्तिभिः ॥ ७ ॥
sarvadevātmako hyeṣa tejasvī raśmi-bhāvanaḥ |
eṣa devāsuragaṇān lokān pāti gabhastibhiḥ || 7 ||
Indeed, He is the very embodiment of all Gods. He is self-luminous and sustains all with his rays. He nourishes and energizes the inhabitants of all the worlds as well as the hosts of gods and demons with his radiant rays.
VERSE 8
एष ब्रह्मा च विष्णुश्च शिवः स्कन्दः प्रजापतिः ।
महेन्द्रो धनदः कालो यमः सोमो ह्यपां पतिः ॥ ८ ॥
eṣa brahmā ca viṣṇuśca śivaḥ skandaḥ prajāpatiḥ |
mahendro dhanadaḥ kālo yamaḥ somo hyapāṃ patiḥ || 8 ||
He is Brahmā the creator, Viṣṇu the sustainer, Śiva the destroyer, Skanda the son of Śiva, Prajāpati the progenitor, mighty Indra king of heaven, Kubera lord of wealth, Kāla eternal time, Yama lord of death, Soma the moon, and Varuṇa lord of the sea.
VERSE 9
पितरो वसवः साध्या ह्यश्विनौ मरुतो मनुः ।
वायु-र्वह्निः प्रजाप्राण ऋतु कर्ता प्रभाकरः ॥ ९ ॥
pitaro vasavaḥ sādhyā hyaśvinau maruto manuḥ |
vāyu-rvahniḥ prajāprāṇa ṛtu kartā prabhākaraḥ || 9 ||
He is the Pitṛs (ancestors), the eight Vasus, the Sādhyas, the twin Aśvins (physicians of gods), the Maruts, Manu, Vāyu the wind god, Agni the fire god, the life-breath of all beings, the maker of the six seasons, and the giver of light.
VERSE 10
आदित्यः सविता सूर्यः खगः पूषा गभस्तिमान् ।
सुवर्णसदृशो भानु-र्हिरण्यरेता दिवाकरः ॥ १० ॥
ādityaḥ savitā sūryaḥ khagaḥ pūṣā gabhastimān |
suvarṇasadṛśo bhānu-rhiraṇyaretā divākaraḥ || 10 ||
He is Āditya (son of Aditi), Savitā (the stimulator), Sūrya (the supreme light), Khaga (who traverses the sky), Pūṣā (the nourisher), Gabhastimān (possessor of brilliant rays), brilliant as gold, Bhānu (the radiant one), Hiraṇyaretā (of golden seed), and Divākara (the maker of daylight).
VERSE 11
हरिदश्वः सहस्रार्चिः सप्तसप्ति-मरीचिमान् ।
तिमिरोन्मथनः शम्भुस्त्वष्टा मार्तण्ड अंशुमान् ॥ ११ ॥
haridaśvaḥ sahasrārciḥ saptasapti-marīcimān |
timironmathanaḥ śambhustvāṣṭā mārtaṇḍa aṃśumān || 11 ||
He whose chariot is yoked with seven horses, who shines with a thousand rays, is the destroyer of darkness, the giver of happiness and prosperity, the mitigator of suffering, and the infuser of life. He is the omnipresent one pervading all with immeasurable rays.
VERSE 12
हिरण्यगर्भः शिशिरस्तपनो भास्करो रविः ।
अग्निगर्भोऽदितेः पुत्रः शङ्खः शिशिरनाशनः ॥ १२ ॥
hiraṇyagarbhaḥ śiśirastapano bhāskaro raviḥ |
agnigarbho'diteḥ putraḥ śaṅkhaḥ śiśiranāśanaḥ || 12 ||
He is Hiraṇyagarbha (of golden womb), the destroyer of cold, snow and fog, the illuminator Ravi, bearer of fire and conch, son of Aditi, and the remover of ignorance.
VERSE 13
व्योमनाथस्तमोभेदी ऋग्यजुस्सामपारगः ।
घनवृष्टिरपां मित्रो विन्ध्यवीथीप्लवङ्गमः ॥ १३ ॥
vyomanāthastamobhedī ṛgyajussāmapāragaḥ |
ghanavṛṣṭirapāṃ mitro vindhyavīthīplavaṅgamaḥ || 13 ||
He is the lord of the firmament, remover of darkness, master of the three Vedas (Ṛg, Yajur, Sāma), friend of the waters causing abundant rain, who swiftly courses south of the Vindhya mountains and sports in the Brahma Nāḍī.
VERSE 14
आतपी मण्डली मृत्युः पिङ्गलः सर्वतापनः ।
कविर्विश्वो महातेजाः रक्तः सर्वभवोद्भवः ॥ १४ ॥
ātapī maṇḍalī mṛtyuḥ piṅgalaḥ sarvatāpanaḥ |
kavirviśvo mahātejāḥ raktaḥ sarvabhavodbhavaḥ || 14 ||
Circular in form and colored in yellow and red hues, intensely brilliant and energetic — He is the giver of heat, the cause of all work, of life and death. He is the destroyer of all and the omniscient one sustaining the universe and all action.
VERSE 15
नक्षत्रग्रहताराणा-मधिपो विश्वभावनः ।
तेजसामपि तेजस्वी द्वादशात्मन्नमोऽस्तु ते ॥ १५ ॥
nakṣatragrahatārāṇā-madhipo viśvabhāvanaḥ |
tejasāmapi tejasvī dvādaśātmannamo'stu te || 15 ||
He is the lord of constellations, stars and planets, and the origin of everything in the universe. Salutations to Āditya who appears in twelve forms — the twelve months of the year — whose glory is described in his twelve names.
VERSE 16
नमः पूर्वाय गिरये पश्चिमायाद्रये नमः ।
ज्योतिर्गणानां पतये दिनाधिपतये नमः ॥ १६ ॥
namaḥ pūrvāya giraye paścimāyādraye namaḥ |
jyotirgaṇānāṃ pataye dinādhibhūtaye namaḥ || 16 ||
Salutations to the Lord of the eastern mountain (sunrise) and the western mountain (sunset). Salutations to the lord of all stellar bodies and to the lord of daylight.
VERSE 17
जयाय जयभद्राय हर्यश्वाय नमो नमः ।
नमो नमः सहस्रांशो आदित्याय नमो नमः ॥ १७ ॥
jayāya jayabhadrāya haryaśvāya namo namaḥ |
namo namaḥ sahasrāṃśo ādityāya namo namaḥ || 17 ||
O Lord of a thousand rays, son of Aditi! Salutations to you, the bestower of victory and auspiciousness and prosperity. Salutations to the one with coloured horses carrying his chariot.
VERSE 18
नम उग्राय वीराय साङ्गाय नमो नमः ।
नमः पद्मप्रबोधाय मार्तण्डाय नमो नमः ॥ १८ ॥
nama ugrāya vīrāya sāraṅgāya namo namaḥ |
namaḥ padmaprabodhāya mārtaṇḍāya namo namaḥ || 18 ||
Salutations to Mārtaṇḍa — the terrible and fierce one, the mighty hero, the one who travels swiftly. Salutations to the one whose appearance makes the lotus blossom and awakens the lotus in the heart.
VERSE 19
ब्रह्मेशानाच्युतेशाय सूर्यायादित्यवर्चसे ।
भास्वते सर्वभक्षाय रौद्राय वपुषे नमः ॥ १९ ॥
brahmeśānācyuteśāya sūryāyādityavarcase |
bhāsvate sarvabhakṣāya raudrāya vapuṣe namaḥ || 19 ||
Salutations to the lord of Brahmā, Śiva and Viṣṇu; to Sūrya the sun god who by his power and effulgence is both the illuminator and devourer of all; and whose form is fierce like Rudra.
VERSE 20
तमोघ्नाय हिमघ्नाय शत्रुघ्नायामितात्मने ।
कृतघ्नघ्नाय देवाय ज्योतिषां पतये नमः ॥ २० ॥
tamoghnāya himaghnāya śatrughnāyāmitātmane |
kṛtaghnaghnāya devāya jyotiṣāṃ pataye namaḥ || 20 ||
Salutations to the dispeller of darkness, the destroyer of cold, fog and snow, the exterminator of foes, the one of immeasurable extent. Salutations also to the annihilator of the ungrateful and to the lord of all stellar bodies — the first amongst all the lights of the universe.
VERSE 21
तप्तचामीकराभाय वह्नये विश्वकर्मणे ।
नमस्तमोऽभिनिघ्नाय रुचये लोकसाक्षिणे ॥ २१ ॥
taptacāmīkarābhāya vahnaye viśvakarmaṇe |
namastamo'bhinighnāya rucaye lokasākṣiṇe || 21 ||
Salutations to the Lord shining like molten gold, who is the transcendental fire of supreme knowledge, who destroys the darkness of ignorance, and who is the cosmic witness of all the merits and demerits of the denizens of the universe. Salutations to Viśvakarma — the architect of the universe and the cause of all creation.

Phalashruti — Verses 22–31

▾
The fruits of this hymn — and the victory of Śrī Rāma
VERSE 22
नाशयत्येष वै भूतं तदेव सृजति प्रभुः ।
पायत्येष तपत्येष वर्षत्येष गभस्तिभिः ॥ २२ ॥
nāśayatyeṣa vai bhūtaṃ tadeva sṛjati prabhuḥ |
pāyatyeṣa tapatyeṣa varṣatyeṣa gabhastibhiḥ || 22 ||
He alone creates, sustains and destroys all that has come into being. He consumes the waters with his rays, heats them up, and sends them down again as rain.
VERSE 23
एष सुप्तेषु जागर्ति भूतेषु परिनिष्ठितः ।
एष एवाग्निहोत्रं च फलं चैवाग्निहोत्रिणाम् ॥ २३ ॥
eṣa supteṣu jāgarti bhūteṣu pariniṣṭhitaḥ |
eṣa evāgnihotraṃ ca phalaṃ caivāgnihotriṇām || 23 ||
The Lord abides in the heart of all beings, keeping awake when they are asleep. Verily, he is the Agnihotra — the sacrificial fire — and also the fruit gained by the worshipper of Agnihotra.
VERSE 24
वेदाश्च क्रतवश्चैव क्रतूनां फलमेव च ।
यानि कृत्यानि लोकेषु सर्व एष रविः प्रभुः ॥ २४ ॥
vedāśca kratavaścaiva kratūnāṃ phalameva ca |
yāni kṛtyāni lokeṣu sarva eṣa raviḥ prabhuḥ || 24 ||
The Sun God (Ravi) is the origin and protector of the four Vedas, the sacrifices mentioned in them and the fruits obtained by performing those sacrifices. He is the lord of all action in this universe and decides the universal path.
VERSE 25
एनमापत्सु कृच्छ्रेषु कान्तारेषु भयेषु च ।
कीर्तयन् पुरुषः कश्चिन्नावसीदति राघव ॥ २५ ॥
enamāpatsu kṛcchreṣu kāntāreṣu bhayeṣu ca |
kīrtayan puruṣaḥ kaścinnāvasīdati rāghava || 25 ||
O Rāghava — scion of the Raghu dynasty — any person who sings the glories of Sūrya in great difficulties, during affliction, while lost in the wilderness, or when beset with fear, shall not come to grief.
VERSE 26
पूजयस्वैनमेकाग्रे देवदेवं जगत्पतिम् ।
एतत् त्रिगुणितं जप्त्वा युद्धेषु विजयिष्यसि ॥ २६ ॥
pūjayasvainamekāgre devadevaṃ jagatpatim |
etat triguṇitaṃ japtvā yuddheṣu vijayiṣyasi || 26 ||
Worship this lord of the universe, the God of all gods, with concentrated mind and devotion. By reciting this hymn (Āditya Hṛdayam) three times, you shall emerge victorious in battle.
VERSE 27
अस्मिन् क्षणे महाबाहो रावणं त्वं वधिष्यसि ।
एवमुक्त्वा तदाऽगस्त्यो जगाम च यथागतम् ॥ २७ ॥
asmin kṣaṇe mahābāho rāvaṇaṃ tvaṃ vadhiṣyasi |
evamuktvā tadā'gastyo jagāma ca yathāgatam || 27 ||
O mighty-armed one, you shall triumph over Rāvaṇa this very moment! Having thus blessed Lord Rāma and predicted that He would slay the demon Rāvaṇa, sage Agastya took leave and returned to his original place.
VERSE 28
एतच्छ्रुत्वा महातेजा नष्टशोकोऽभवत्तदा ।
धारयामास सुप्रीतो राघवः प्रयतात्मवान् ॥ २८ ॥
etacchrutvā mahātejā naṣṭaśoko'bhavattadā |
dhārayāmāsa supréto rāghavaḥ prayatātmavān || 28 ||
Having heard this, the great warrior Rāghava was filled with delight and became free from grief. His clouds of worry thus dispelled, the lustrous Lord Rāma retained this hymn in his memory with composed mind, ready to chant the Āditya Hṛdayam.
VERSE 29
आदित्यं प्रेक्ष्य जप्त्वा तु परं हर्षमवाप्तवान् ।
त्रिराचम्य शुचिर्भूत्वा धनुरादाय वीर्यवान् ॥ २९ ॥
ādityaṃ prekṣya japtvā tu paraṃ harṣamavāptavān |
triracamya śucirbhūtvā dhanurādāya vīryavān || 29 ||
Having performed Ācamanam (sipping water thrice) and being purified, Rāma gazed at the Sun with devotion and recited the hymn Āditya Hṛdayam three times. That great hero Rāghava was thrilled and lifted his bow.
VERSE 30
रावणं प्रेक्ष्य हृष्टात्मा युद्धाय समुपागमत् ।
सर्वयत्नेन महता वधे तस्य धृतोऽभवत् ॥ ३० ॥
rāvaṇaṃ prekṣya hṛṣṭātmā yuddhāya samupāgamat |
sarvayatnena mahatā vadhe tasya dhṛto'bhavat || 30 ||
Lord Rāma, thus cheered, seeing Rāvaṇa coming to fight, put forth all his effort with an unwavering determination to defeat him.
VERSE 31
अथ रवि-रवद-न्निरीक्ष्य रामं
मुदितमनाः परमं प्रहृष्यमाणः ।
निशिचरपति-संक्षयं विदित्वा
सुरगण-मध्यगतो वचस्त्वरेति ॥ ३१ ॥
atha ravi-ravada-nnirīkṣya rāmaṃ
muditamanāḥ paramaṃ prahṛṣyamāṇaḥ |
niśicarapati-saṅkṣayaṃ viditvā
suragaṇa-madhyagato vacastvareti || 31 ||
Then, knowing that the destruction of Rāvaṇa was near, the Sun God Āditya — surrounded by all the gods in heaven — looked at Rāma with a delighted mind and exclaimed: "Hurry up! Be quick!"
|| iti Āditya Hṛdayam Mantrasya ||
Thus ends the Mantra Āditya Hṛdayam in praise of the Sun God,
recounted in the Yuddha Kāṇḍa of Vālmīki Rāmāyaṇa 🌞
